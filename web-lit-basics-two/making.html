The Web Is for Making

Try a project and make something with the Web!

You may want to begin your project by sketching and paper prototyping your plan. "Paper prototyping" lets you use paper cut-outs to create a quick and inexpensive model of whatever you want to design or build. You can see which parts fit together and which might need to be repositioned or changed before you make your "final" project. You can paper prototype a web page or screen of a video game, as well as 3D models of things you want to build. See an example of paper prototyping here!

Beginner

  • Build a giant cardboard marble maze with Instructables.
  • Caption an image to remix a Meme with DIY.org or Mozilla's Thimble.
  • Launch this foam falcon with Make.

Intermediate

  • Assemble this cardboard mini-foosball game from Make .
  • Make a cardboard automaton with the Exploratorium.

Advanced

  • Watch "Turn your Smartphone into a 3D Hologram," and then see how to recycle plastic packaging so you don't have to find and cut up a CD case.

Younger makers: Make sure you get adult help with all cutting tools.
