Audience

Materials

  • Pens and pencils
  • Paper
  • An Internet-connected computer
  • A web browser

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize when a website or service is asking for private, personal information.
  • Explain how websites and services use cookies to track users' movement online.
  • Use online tools to monitor cookies tracking Internet use.
  • Identify and explain strong and weak Internet privacy habits.
  • Explain the importance of strong Internet privacy habits to others.

Expanded Privacy Kit | Privacy


Privacy Agenda

Made by Stacy Martin, Senior Data Privacy Manager at Mozilla.

Learners will develop strategies to safeguard their information online and discover how to monitor websites that track their browser histories using cookies, learning about privacy.

4 hours

  • Introduction

    Think of devloping Internet privacy habits as somthing similar to taking a self-defense class. As you practice and level-up your Internet privacy skills, you will find ways to avoid harmful interactions online and to protect yourself from attacks on your privacy.

    This Privacy Teaching Kit has been built to help learners engage, explore and evaluate their understanding of online privacy. You can remix the order of activites to suit yourself or your group of learners best.

  • Agenda

    1. If it's personal offline, it's personal online - 30 minutes
    2. Spectrogram for Understanding Privacy - 15 minutes
    3. Terms and Conditions May Apply and Discussion - 1 hours and 45 minutes
    4. Privacy Game - 30 minutes
    5. What's Your Data Worth? - 10 minutes
    6. Ready or Not? - 10 minutes
    7. EXIF Tracer - 10 minutes
    8. Tracking cookies with Lightbeam - 45 minutes
    9. What's your elevator speech? - 15 minutes
